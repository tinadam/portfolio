Knew News, an augmented reality news app



We made a news source aggregator app with an augmented reality (AR) component that allows users to quickly look up key information and background context



Timeline: 3 weeks
Project Context: Group work for UX/UI bootcamp
My Roles: UX research, UX design, UI design
Primary Tools: Figma

Researching



background
In the current digital age, we are faced with a multitude of ways to consume the news. As we flip through a newsletter or browse a mobile app, we might encounter terms that we'd like to look up for deeper understanding and background context. At this point, we usually decide if we want to put in the effort to Google or move on. We were wondering if we could create an app that can help alleviate this burden of research for users.





proto
persona






user
research
plan
We wanted to understand why users spent more or less time reading the news. Specifically, we wanted to:
  • Understand how users feel about the information they read

  • Discover the different tools users utilize to read the news, and how they feel about each tool

  • See if AR would be valuable for users
We carried out five Zoom interviews and surveyed eight other users through Google Forms.





key
takeaways
Based on our affinity diagram results (quick previews shown below), we saw that users
  • Mostly browsed news digitally to stay informed

  • Appreciated succint news

  • Conducted further research to understand details and relationships

  • Felt optimistic but hesitant about the potential of AR technology





Our Google Forms responses corroborated these results. Some recurring quotes of interest included:
“I do worry that my viewpoint is becoming increasingly one-sided”
“[I] check Wikipedia or Google anything that's unfamiliar”
“[AR apps are] neat but I don't really use them. The tech has a lot of potential though I think”





user
persona






problem
statement
How might we design a news app so that our users spend less time looking up information externally?





section
summary
Users generally have to go out of their way to look up further information when they're reading the news. We were wondering if there was a way we could implement AR technology to support users. We saw that our typical user likes short and simple news articles, uses Google or Wikipedia for deeper understanding, and is lightly familiar with AR technology.

usability
testing
We got five users to perform three tasks in order to understand how intuitive it was for them to scan an unknown term and navigate the profile page.

Key takeaways from testing were that users:
  • Felt the home screen was overwhelming

  • Would like in-depth guidance during first use, especially with AR feature

  • Enjoyed personalization aspect, but also valued simplicity
Therefore, we scaled back on the home page content and created a tutorial for the AR feature.

Reflecting



future
direction
Our team would've loved to expand on socialization aspects, recommended content and further personalization, and working with other news formats (eg, podcasts, videos, audio files).

Our user research showed that a huge part of reading and understanding the news is its social component, so we would've enjoyed bringing that to life.





final
thoughts
We stumbled across many surprises along the way and learned their value. For instance, we had to be careful with AR since our users did not always react warmly to this technology. We also had to completely redo our home screen several times, which reminded us of the importance of iterating. Otherwise, this project was so much fun to really dive into especially since reading the news is something so relevant and integral to our daily lives.
